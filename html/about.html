Marvel and DC are both comic book publishers that have been creating and publishing comic books for several decades. Marvel Comics, founded in 1939, is known for its vast interconnected universe of characters, including the Avengers, the X-Men, and the Fantastic Four, who all exist within the same continuity and often crossover with one another. DC Comics, established in 1934, is known for its iconic characters like Batman, Superman, and Wonder Woman, and has a more diverse collection of franchises and universes, each with its own continuity. Both companies have had a significant impact on popular culture and have produced numerous successful movies, TV shows, and video games based on their characters.

Marvel

Marvel is a brand name and entertainment company specializing in the creation and production of comic books, movies, TV shows, and merchandise based on its vast library of characters and stories.

Founded in 1939 as Timely Publications, and later known as Atlas Comics, it was rebranded as Marvel Comics in the 1960s and has since become one of the biggest and most recognizable brands in the comic book industry, with characters like Spider-Man, The X-Men, Iron Man, and The Avengers among its many popular franchises.

Marvel's impact has extended beyond the comic book industry and has become a major player in the entertainment world, producing successful films and TV shows under the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) brand.

DC

DC Comics is a American comic book publisher founded in 1934, known for its iconic superhero characters such as Superman, Batman, Wonder Woman, The Flash, Aquaman, and many others.

DC has played a major role in the evolution of the comic book industry, and its characters have been adapted into various media, including television shows, movies, and video games.

The company is now a subsidiary of Warner Bros. Entertainment, a division of AT&T's WarnerMedia.
